\chapter{Introduction}
%==============================================================================

This report evaluates transferability, adaptation behavior, data efficiency, and robustness of ImageNet pre-trained CNN backbones on the AID 30-class aerial scene classification task.

The study answers the following assignment questions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Which architecture transfers best and why?
  \item Which model is most robust under corruption?
  \item Which layers encode the most transferable semantics?
  \item When does fine-tuning improve or degrade robustness?
  \item How does parameter-efficiency relate to accuracy?
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}
\textbf{Separability:} All three backbones show strong class structure, with t-SNE exhibiting better local clustering than PCA. DenseNet121 shows comparatively tighter cluster compactness for several classes, matching its stronger macro metrics.\\
\textbf{Transfer quality:} DenseNet121 achieved the best linear-probe performance (val acc 0.9205, macro F1 0.9155), followed by ResNet50 (0.8913, 0.8847), then Inception-v3 (0.8821, 0.8750). Training curves indicate rapid early convergence for all models and stable validation after approximately 10 epochs.\\
\textbf{Failure cases:} Confusion matrices suggest persistent confusion among visually similar urban classes (for example, residential/commercial categories), indicating that frozen backbones still retain some class-overlap under domain-specific aerial textures.

\section{Scenario 2 Analysis}
Across all three backbones, moving from linear probe to partial/complete unfreezing improves validation accuracy, but the best strategy is architecture-dependent. ResNet50 and DenseNet121 peak at \textit{last\_block} (0.9602 and 0.9545), while Inception-v3 peaks at \textit{full\_ft} (0.9403). This indicates that deeper adaptation generally helps transfer, but fully unfreezing is not always optimal.

Convergence plots show that train loss decreases faster with larger unfrozen fractions, while validation loss stability depends on strategy. Selective\_20 often gives a strong compromise: significantly higher accuracy than linear probe at much lower trainable percentage than full fine-tuning.

Fine-tuning can reduce robustness when early and mid-level filters over-specialize to the training distribution. This typically appears when the train-val gap widens under full\_ft despite low train loss, indicating adaptation of generic ImageNet features toward dataset-specific cues that may not transfer under shifts.

\chapter{Scenario 3: Few-Shot Learning Analysis}
%==============================================================================

\section{Objective}
Evaluate data efficiency at 100\%, 20\%, and 5\% training data.

\section{Protocol, Parameters, and Fine-Tuning Mode}
\textbf{Few-shot protocol:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Backbone set: ResNet50, Inception-v3, DenseNet121.
  \item Fractions: \{1.0, 0.2, 0.05\} of the original training split.
  \item Subset sampling: deterministic with seed 1337; 20\% is sampled from 100\%, and 5\% is sampled from the same run's 20\% subset.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Optimization parameters:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Epochs: 20 (fixed for all few-shot runs).
  \item Batch size: 32.
  \item Input size: 299.
  \item Optimizer: AdamW, learning rate $1\times10^{-3}$, weight decay $1\times10^{-4}$.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Fine-tuning used in Scenario 3:} \texttt{selective\_20} for all backbones.
This unfreezes the classifier head and only the last \textasciitilde20\% of backbone parameters (by cumulative parameter count), while earlier backbone parameters remain frozen. Observed trainable fractions are 23.66\% (ResNet50), 21.49\% (Inception-v3), and 21.55\% (DenseNet121).

\section{Metrics}
Relative performance drop:
\begin{equation}
\Delta = \frac{\mathrm{Acc}_{100\%} - \mathrm{Acc}_{5\%}}{\mathrm{Acc}_{100\%}}
\end{equation}

Training-validation gap:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Gap} = \mathrm{Acc}_{\mathrm{train}} - \mathrm{Acc}_{\mathrm{val}}
\end{equation}

\section{Objective}
Compare clean-vs-corrupted accuracy under Gaussian noise, motion blur, and brightness shift.

\section{Settings and Evaluation Protocol}
\textbf{Checkpoint source:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Scenario 1 best \texttt{linear\_probe} checkpoints (one per backbone).
  \item Scenario 2 best checkpoints for \texttt{selective\_20}, \texttt{last\_block}, and \texttt{full\_ft}.
  \item Duplicate \texttt{linear\_probe} from Scenario 2 was excluded to avoid repeated evaluation.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Corruption settings:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Gaussian noise: $\sigma \in \{0.05, 0.10, 0.20\}$.
  \item Motion blur: horizontal and vertical blur, kernel size $k=9$.
  \item Brightness shift: additive deltas $\Delta b \in \{-0.20, -0.10, +0.10, +0.20\}$.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{How corruptions are applied:} corruptions are injected \emph{only at evaluation time} on validation images with transform order
\[
\texttt{Resize} \rightarrow \texttt{ToTensor} \rightarrow \texttt{Corruption} \rightarrow \texttt{Normalize}.
\]
Training data and training procedure are unchanged; no additional training is done for Scenario 4.

\section{Metrics}
Corruption error:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Corruption\ Error} = 1 - \mathrm{Acc}_{\mathrm{corrupted}}
\end{equation}

Relative robustness:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Relative\ Robustness} = \frac{\mathrm{Acc}_{\mathrm{corrupted}}}{\mathrm{Acc}_{\mathrm{clean}}}
\end{equation}

\section{Scenario 5 Discussion}
Across all three architectures, probe accuracy increases with depth, confirming semantic abstraction in deeper representations. Final-depth features are most transferable for AID classification: ResNet50 (0.9643), Inception-v3 (0.9507), and DenseNet121 (0.9500).

Early-layer features retain low-level texture/edge cues and show greater class overlap in PCA, especially for Inception-v3 where early-depth accuracy is only 0.4904. Mid layers improve separation as object-part and layout cues emerge, while final layers produce the highest compactness for many classes and the best linear separability.

Feature norm trends are architecture-dependent and should not be interpreted as quality by magnitude alone. DenseNet121 has very high early norms but still gains substantial separability at deeper depths; Inception-v3 shows increasing norms with depth and a large separability jump from early to mid/final; ResNet50 peaks in mid-layer norm yet achieves best separability at final depth. This indicates that representation hierarchy (structure and invariance), not raw norm scale, is the key driver of transferability and robustness.

\chapter{Cross-Scenario Analysis}

\section{Accuracy and Efficiency Trade-offs}
Cross-scenario trends from \texttt{results/tables/accuracy\_comparison.csv}, \texttt{results/tables/robustness\_comparison.csv}, and \texttt{results/tables/parameter\_efficiency.csv} show a consistent trade-off:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Highest absolute clean accuracy:} fine-tuning with deeper adaptation (mostly \texttt{last\_block} or \texttt{full\_ft}) reaches the top clean validation scores.
  \item \textbf{Highest parameter efficiency:} linear probing gives far higher accuracy per million trainable parameters than full fine-tuning.
  \item \textbf{Data efficiency:} at 100\% data in few-shot protocol, \texttt{selective\_20} remains close to fine-tune peaks while training far fewer parameters.
  \item \textbf{Robustness under corruption:} clean-accuracy leaders are not automatically robust to severe noise; corruption sensitivity depends on both architecture and adaptation strategy.
\end{itemize}

Numerically, DenseNet121 and ResNet50 obtain near-best clean accuracy with \texttt{last\_block}, while Inception-v3 prefers \texttt{full\_ft}. Under corruption, mean relative robustness is highest for Inception-v3 (about 0.799), whereas DenseNet121 and ResNet50 suffer larger worst-case drops under strong Gaussian noise.

\section{Unexpected Finding}
An unexpected pattern is that high clean accuracy does not guarantee resilience to severe pixel noise. For both ResNet50 and DenseNet121, some corruption-mode checkpoints show near-random accuracy under Gaussian perturbations at high severity, despite strong clean performance. This reveals that adaptation can over-specialize representations toward clean-domain statistics and become brittle to stochastic high-frequency shifts.

\section{Negative/Failure Case}
The strongest failure case occurs for Gaussian noise (especially $\sigma=0.2$), where corruption error spikes and relative robustness collapses. This failure is much stronger than brightness shift degradation and generally stronger than motion blur, indicating a systematic vulnerability to additive noise rather than a generic failure across all distribution shifts.

\chapter{Conclusions}
%==============================================================================

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Best transfer architecture (clean task):} DenseNet121 and ResNet50 are strongest overall on AID with deep adaptation; Inception-v3 is competitive but more sensitive to scenario/strategy.
  \item \textbf{Most robust architecture (Scenario 4 aggregate):} Inception-v3 shows the highest mean relative robustness, while DenseNet121 can achieve the best mean corrupted accuracy under selected checkpoints.
  \item \textbf{Most transferable depth:} final-layer features consistently give the highest probe accuracy, confirming increasing semantic abstraction with depth.
  \item \textbf{Fine-tuning and robustness:} deeper fine-tuning improves clean accuracy but can degrade robustness to strong Gaussian noise when representations overfit clean-domain statistics.
  \item \textbf{Parameter-efficiency:} linear probing is the most efficient by trainable-parameter budget; selective unfreezing offers a strong middle ground between efficiency and accuracy.
\end{itemize}

ImageNet-pretrained CNNs transfer strongly to aerial scene classification, but performance depends critically on adaptation depth and evaluation conditions. Overall, the best practical compromise is strategy-dependent: use parameter-efficient probing/selective tuning for budgeted deployment, and deeper adaptation with explicit robustness regularization when peak accuracy is required.
